What I do

In my role as the Lead Data Science Manager at Motivate Lab, an educational research lab, I sit at the intersection of academia and industry, publishing in peer-reviewed, scientific journals and making data-driven decisions to promote our mission of improving people's lives through rigorous motivation research.

At the heart of my responsibilities is driving professional development for college faculty. I am committed to advancing the knowledge and skills of educators, ensuring they stay at the forefront of pedagogical practices through evidence-based insights derived from our research. Leveraging my statistical training and previous experience as a faculty member at a small liberal arts college, I bring a unique perspective to my work.

In a previous role, I served as Assistant Professor of Psychology in Developmental and Experimental Research at Maryville College, where I taught courses in statistics, research methodology, and developmental psychology. As a teacher, I take pride in my ability to teach complex statistical and research skills by combining conceptual, visual, and mathematical explanations, and I always tried to share with students my excitement and commitment to being a lifelong learner.

Expertise

I have worked with a range of statistical modeling techniques: linear models, generalized linear models, hierarchical models, path analysis, latent variable models, and time series analysis. I am most comfortable working in Python and R, though I have also worked with other programming languages, including JavaScript, SPSS, Stan, and SAS.

I am a Bayesian at heart, though I spend considerable time working in a frequentist framework. Recently, I have been studying and using various machine learning algorithms to expand my statistical repertoire.

Academic Background

I received my MA and PhD in Experimental Psychology from The University of Alabama and my BA in Psychology from Rivier College (a small liberal arts college in New Hampshire). My educational experiences have shaped my belief in the transformative power of education, the importance of continued professional development, and the benefit of learning from those with a different experience than your own.

Image Classification for Technical Indicators

We created a sample of financial time-series images (candlestick charts and line graphs) and developed a machine learning image classification algorithm to identify previously labeled technical indicators (e.g., RSI).
